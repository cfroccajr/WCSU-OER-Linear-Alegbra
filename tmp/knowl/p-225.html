\begin{equation*} proj_{\vec{v}}\vec{w}=\frac{\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}}{\vec{v}\cdot\vec{v}}\vec{v}=\left\lt\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right\gt. \end{equation*}

The vector

\begin{equation*} \vec{w}-proj_{\vec{v}}\vec{w}=\left\lt-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right\gt. \end{equation*}

is an orthogonal complement of the projection; it is orthogonal to \(\vec{v}\) and we can use it to replace \(\vec{w}\text{.}\)
