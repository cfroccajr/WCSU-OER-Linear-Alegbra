Definition 3.5.1.

A basis \(\mathcal{B}\) is said to be an orthogonal basis if each pair of vectors in \(\mathcal{B}\text{,}\) \(\vec{\beta}_i\) and \(\vec{\beta}_j\text{,}\) are orthogonal 1 . If in addition \(||\vec{\beta}_i||=1\) for all \(i\) then we say that the basis is orthonormal.
