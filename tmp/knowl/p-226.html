Consider

\begin{align*} a\vec{v}+b\vec{w}\amp = a\vec{v}+b\vec{w}\\ \amp = a\vec{v}+b\, \left(\vec{w}-proj_{\vec{v}}\vec{w}+proj_{\vec{v}}\vec{w}\right)\\ \amp = a\vec{v}+b\, proj_{\vec{v}}\vec{w}+b\, \left(\vec{w}-proj_{\vec{v}}\vec{w}\right)\\ \amp = a\vec{v}+\frac{b}{2}\vec{v}+b\, \left(\vec{w}-proj_{\vec{v}}\vec{w}\right),\\ \amp = \left(a+\frac{b}{2}\right)\vec{v}+b\, \left(\vec{w}-proj_{\vec{v}}\vec{w}\right), \end{align*}

so we can systematically replace any linear combination of \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\) with a combination of \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}-proj_{\vec{v}}\vec{w}\text{.}\) This is the Gram-Schmidt Orthogonalization process.
